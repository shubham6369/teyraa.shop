Est. 1924

A Century of Chasing Perfection

Three generations. One obsession. The relentless pursuit of the perfect second.

1924

The First Movement

In the quiet hamlet of Le Sentier, Henri-Louis Teyraa assembled the family's first in-house movement by candlelight. Fourteen components, hand-filed, hand-fitted — and accurate to within five seconds a day. A miracle for its time.

1961

The Chronograph Revolution

André Teyraa, Henri's son, unveiled the Calibre 61 — the world's first column-wheel chronograph with a lateral clutch housed in a case thinner than 10mm. Worn by jet pilots and race champions alike, it rewrote what precision meant.

1985

The Age of Complications

The Grande Complication Perpétuel — a moonphase, perpetual calendar, and tourbillon in a single case — was presented at Baselworld. It took a team of seven watchmakers four years to complete. Only 25 were ever made.

2012

The Digital Renaissance

The third generation, led by Isabelle Teyraa, digitized the archive and opened the first Teyraa Maison in Mumbai — bringing Swiss legacy to the world's most time-conscious culture. The collection expanded to 48 references across four pillars.

Now

Your Chapter Begins

Today, Teyraa crafts fewer than 3,000 pieces per year. Each one individually regulated, tested, and documented. Each one waiting to begin a new chapter in a new story — yours.

"We do not make watches. We author the instruments through which life is remembered."

— Isabelle Teyraa-Moreau, Creative Director & Third-Generation Watchmaker
